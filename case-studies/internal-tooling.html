Internal Tooling and Automation for IT Operations

Duration: 12 months
Role: Systems Analyst
Team Size: 3

Overview

Developed internal tooling to enhance operational efficiency and automate recurring IT tasks. The goal was to reduce manual overhead, streamline infrastructure management, and boost client service reliability across the company. Efforts were largely focused on Python and Go-based automations.

Challenge

The organization faced high volumes of routine administrative tasks, inconsistent system configurations, and inefficient patching practices. Specific challenges included:

  • Manual provisioning and configuration leading to inconsistencies
  • Delayed patch rollouts increasing vulnerability exposure
  • Time-consuming Office 365 account and license management
  • Lack of centralized scripts or monitoring tools for multiple clients

Solution

Delivered a suite of internal tools and automations to standardize and scale IT operations. Notable solutions included:

  • Developed an automated patch management scheduler to enforce consistent updates across all client machines
  • Created Python scripts for Office 365 account provisioning and license management
  • Engineered system configuration and imaging scripts for quick deployment of devices and network hardware
  • Implemented optimization workflows for resource management, leading to faster applications and improved UX
  • Conducted systems architecture reviews to proactively identify and resolve performance and security gaps

Results

The automation initiatives delivered measurable improvements across operational efficiency and reliability:

  • Significantly reduced downtime with automated, scheduled patching
  • Streamlined Office 365 operations, saving several hours per week on administrative tasks
  • Faster device deployment through standardized provisioning workflows
  • Noticeable increase in system responsiveness and end-user productivity
  • Proactive issue resolution through system audits and tooling improvements
